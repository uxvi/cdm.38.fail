# Modernization and Recognition Act, 2025

In recognition that certain resolutions of the National Forum no longer serve the public interest or have become inoperative, this bill seeks their repeal in accordance with Section 50(2)(a) of the Constitution. In addition, this document provides supplicant legislation regarding honorifics and capital management.

---

## 1. Title and Commencement
1) This Act may be cited as the Modernization and Recognition Act, 2025.
2) This Act and all documents annexed come into effect upon being signed by the President and Vice President.

## 2. Repeal of Enactments
1) The following enactments are hereby repealed:
(a) IceCarim is C.O.O.L. Resolution, 2024
(b) Setra Maria Capital Resolution, 2024
(c) Law Summary Resolution, 2024
(d) Stations Act, 1 of 2024
(e) Dual Nationality Act, 2 of 2024

## 3. Enactment of Supplementary Legislation
1) The annexed documents appended to this act are enforced upon this document's signing. Listed in order:
* The Honor and History Act, 2025
* Bestowment of "Constitutional Architect" to IceCarim
* Montefiore Capital Resolution, 2025
* Montefiore Independent Agencies Resolution, 2025

## Annex A:

### The Honor and History Act, 2025

This document establishes a national framework for the conferral of honors and titles, and expands upon _Section 81(2)(g)_ of the constitution to designate historically and culturally significant lands as public assets in service of the Nation.

#### 1. Title and Commencement
1) This Act may be cited as the Honor and History Act, 2025.

#### 2. Designation of National Land
1) The Minister of the Interior may, with public notice and in consultation with the Cabinet, designate land within the Republic as a historic or recreational area for public benefit.
2) A valid designation must be:
(a) Consistent with the cultural, historical, or ecological aims of the Republic;
(b) Accompanied by a written statement justifying the designation.

3) 
(a) Where the designated land lies within a city, the local government concerned must be consulted and given a reasonable opportunity to respond before the designation takes effect.
(b) This window is to be no less than 72 hours without notarized consent from the local government.
(c)
  (i) If the designation is challenged by the affected or neighboring local government or by public petition, the National Forum is to hold a public meeting and vote weighing the benefit of the designation.
  (ii) This vote is to be decided by simple majority of the forum, and must last a minimum of 24 hours.

#### 3. Registry and Bestowment of Titles 
1) The Cabinet shall, with public notice, determine:
(a) Classes of career and honorary titles;
(b) Eligibility criteria, including duration of service, merit, or distinction;
(c) Modes of nomination and approval.

2) Titles must:
(a) Not confer any power unless otherwise authorized by national law;
(b) Be compatible with constitutional ethical obligations (_Article X_).

## Annex D:

### Montefiore Independent Agency Resolution, 2025

Recognizing the importance of the agency of the individual, and in recognition of the need to uphold _Sections 12-15_ of the constitution; We acknowledge the need for organizations to facilitate the production and distribution of these needs and services.

#### Resolution

1. The Republic asserts the need for:
  * The sale of Tools, Equipment, XP, and Food;
  * Depository facilities for the exchange and processing of ore for currency;
  * Local, state, and international parcel service(s);
  * Legal Counsel & Notary service(s);

2. The Republic approves the creation of the following organizations, provisionally owned in whole by the Forum:
  * Woolworths, Inc.
  * The Cortesian National Foundry
  * The Quickbuild Auto-Delivery System (QBADS)
  * State Legal Service

3. These organizations are designated as Independent Agencies:
(a) Independent Agencies held by the Forum must have their ownership transitioned to a private party when sufficient interest in maintaining the service is voiced.
(b) These agencies may not be dissolved or restricted in the initial defined scope; ownership of each agency requires bearing the responsibility of serving the public’s needs.
(c) These agencies may have their ownership reclaimed by the forum by two-thirds vote.
(d) Profits from these agencies are to be divided by a percentile defined by the treasurer between the national budget and private party.
