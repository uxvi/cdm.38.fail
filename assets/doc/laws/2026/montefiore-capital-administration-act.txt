# Montefiore Capital Administration Act

## 1. Title and Commencement
1. This Act may be cited as the “Montefiore Capital Administration Act”.
2. This law is in effect immediately after passage by the Forum; subsequent amendments are in effect upon the explicit signature of the President.

## 2. Definitions
1. “Bylaw” means a statutory instrument enacted under this Act regulating a matter relating to the Capital.
2. “Administrative authority” means any juristic entity recognized under this Act for the management of the Capital.

## 3. Administrative Authority
1. The National Forum is permitted, with endorsement by the Cabinet, to serve as or appoint an administrative authority for the Capital.
2. An administrative authority—
* a. exercises only functions assigned by law or statutory instrument;
* b. operates subject to the direction and oversight of the Cabinet on matters of its jurisdiction; 
* c. has authority only within the territory of the Capital as defined in Appendix 1; and
* d. does not possess independent executive authority.
3. The Cabinet may amend or revoke any authority granted under this Section at any time.
4. All Capital finances remain vested in the Treasury.
5. All administrative functions concerning the Capital remain vested in the Cabinet.

## 4. Bylaws
1. Bylaws may regulate matters relating to the management of the Capital.
2. A bylaw must—
* a. be consistent with the Constitution and national legislation;
* b. only apply within the territory of the Capital as defined in Appendix 1; and
* c. fall within the scope of this Act.
3. A bylaw may not—
* a. create criminal offenses or define criminal penalties;
* b. impose taxation or compulsory levies unless authorized by national legislation; or
* c. contradict or override Cabinet decisions.
4. Any inconsistent bylaw is inoperative to the extent of the inconsistency.

## 5. Adoption of Bylaws
1. Bylaws may be adopted—
* a. by the Forum.
* b. through the commandeering of a public power in accordance with §44 of the Constitution.
2. All bylaws are adopted only once appended to this bill’s annex through an amendment.

## 6. Advisory Committee
1. The National Forum may establish a Capital Advisory Committee by resolution.
2. The Committee—
* a. serves in an advisory capacity only;
* b. may receive public proposals and petitions; and
* c. may submit recommendations or draft bylaws to the Cabinet or the National Forum.
3. The Committee does not exercise binding authority.

## 7. Public Participation
1. Any person may submit proposals concerning the Capital to the Cabinet, the Committee, or the National Forum.

## 8. Supremacy
1. This Act does not limit the authority of the Cabinet.
2. In the event of conflict, national legislation prevails over bylaws.

## Annex A:

### Montefiore City Property Sale Bylaw

#### 1. Title and Commencement
1) This Bylaw may be cited as the Montefiore Initial Sale Bylaw.

#### 2. Definitions
1) "Party", in this Bylaw, refers specifically to any party specified in Section 2(b) of the Property Title Act.
1) "City property", in this Bylaw, refers to property within the Capital not owned by a party.
2) The "city government", in this Bylaw, refers to the entity serving as an administrative authority, in accordance with Section 3 of the Montefiore Capital Administration Act.

#### 3. Sellable City Property
1) City property may be designated by the city government as--
(a) sellable; or
(b) non-sellable. 
2) Upon designating a property as sellable, the city government must--
(a) publicly list the property, in a manner of their choosing, as sellable; and
(b) assign a price to the property, in accordance with Section 3. 
3) All city property in the Capital is designated as non-sellable by default.

#### 4. Pricing of Sellable City Property
1) All sellable city property must have associated with it a price, rendered in terms of CivMC currency.
2) The price of a sellable city property must be set fairly, taking into account as applicable--
(a) the market price of comparable units in the city;
(b) the size and square footage of the property;
(c) the location of the building, including proximity to important sites; and
(d) the designated or otherwise intended use(s) for the property.
3) Once set in terms of Section 2(2), the city government may update the price of a sellable city property only with cause. 

#### 5. Sale of City Property
1) Except as otherwise specified by this or other bylaws, or by national law, any party may purchase ownership of sellable city property for the associated price.
2) All requests for sale of sellable city property must be processed and either approved or denied by the city government.
3) A sale of sellable city property to a particular party may be denied--
(a) if the request is frivolous or unserious;
(b) if the request is demonstrably malicious towards another party;
(a) if the sale would be demonstrably pernicious to the interests of the public; or
(b) if there is another pending request which--
  (i) offers a higher price; or
  (ii) was made sooner, if the offered prices are the same.

#### 6. First Residence Exception
1) A sellable city property may be purchased for 1 diamond by an individual who--
(a) is a Cortesian national; and
(b) does not currently own any residential property in the Capital.
2) The city government may exercise discretion in determining which sellable city properties may be purchased in accordance with Subsection (1).
3) The city government must make available to any individual who fits the requirements in Subsection (1) at least one property which may be purchased in accordance with Subsection (1).

## Annex B:

### Montefiore Dereliction Policy Bylaw

#### 1. Title and Commencement
1) This Bylaw may be cited as the Montefiore Dereliction Policy Bylaw.

#### 2. Definitions
1) "Party", in this Bylaw, refers specifically to any party specified in Section 2(b) of the Property Title Act.
2) The "city government", in this Bylaw, refers to the entity serving as an administrative authority, in accordance with Section 3 of the Montefiore Capital Administration Act.
3) "Dereliction", in this Bylaw, refers to expropriation of property owned by a party to the city government, without compensation, on the basis of--
(a) extended disuse of the property by the owning party; in addition to
(b) the absence of any reasonable apprehesion that the owning party will resume usage of the property within a reasonable timeframe.

#### 3. Dereliction
1) Property may only be derelicted on the basis described in Section 2(3), specific conditions for which are laid out in Section 4.
2) All derelictions must be initiated and conducted by the city government.
3) For a dereliction to be valid, the city government must make a good-faith effort to inform the owner of the property of its liability to dereliction no more than 7 days before expropriating it.
4) The city government may exercise discretion in determining when and if to exercise dereliction.

#### 4. Conditions for Dereliction
1) A commercial property may be derelicted if the owning party has not used it for commerce, or made a substantive effort to use it for commerce, in 30 days.
2) Any other type of property may be derelicted if no individual belonging to the owning party has entered the property in 30 days.

## Annex C:

### Montefiore Shops Incentive Program Bylaw

#### 1. Title and Commencement
1) This Bylaw may be cited as the Montefiore Shops Incentive Program Bylaw.

#### 2. Incentive Program
1) The Montefiore Shops Incentive Program (hereafter "the Program") is a financial incentive program managed by the Cortesian Bank & Reserve (hereafter "the CB&R") on behalf of the Capital.
2) Any business eligible for the Program in terms of Section 3 may receive payouts from the Program as specified in Section 4, by way of their choice of--
(a) physical diamonds;
(b) Cortesian Labor Notes, converted from diamonds at their present exchange rate; or
(c) promissory diamonds, rendered via a digital payment service.
3) An eligible business remains entitled to request a given payout for up to one month after the payout first becomes available to that business in accordance with Section 4.
4) Any business entitled to receive payouts from the Program must be sent the payouts to which it is entitled by the CB&R on request.

#### 3. Eligibility
1) For a business to receive a payout in terms of this Program, the business must--
(a) be eligible in terms of Subsection (2);
(b) open a shop location in the Capital which is eligible in terms of Subsection (3); and
(c) not be engaging with the Program in bad faith.
2) An eligible business must--
(a) be a for-profit or non-profit business;
(b) have existed for at least three months; 
(c) have at least two employees; 
(d) not share 50% or more of its employees with any other business that has received payouts from the Program; and
(e) maintain at least one shop location outside of the Capital.
3) An eligible shop must--
(a) be the first shop the business has opened in the Capital; 
(b) sell at least three different goods via chestshop; and
(c) be stocked. 

#### 4. Payouts
1) To any business eligible in terms of Section 3, a payout of-- 
(a) 100 diamonds becomes available immediately upon the shop opening to the public.
(a) 100 diamonds becomes available 1 month after the shop opens to the public.
(a) 100 diamonds becomes available 2 months after the shop opens to the public.
(a) 100 diamonds becomes available 3 months after the shop opens to the public.
(a) 50 diamonds becomes available 4 months after the shop opens to the public.
(a) 50 diamonds becomes available 5 months after the shop opens to the public.
